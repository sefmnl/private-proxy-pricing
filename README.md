## Datacenter vs Residential vs ISP: Which One Should You Actually Buy?

Three flavors. Three completely different use cases. Don't overpay for residential when datacenter would do the job, and don't try to scrape Instagram with datacenter IPs and wonder why everything breaks.

**Datacenter private proxies** — fastest, cheapest, easiest to get blocked on aggressive sites. Use these for: SEO tools, public data scraping, market research on sites without heavy bot wals, ad verification on smaller publishers, and anything bandwidth-heavy where speed matters more than stealth.

**Residential proxies** — real ISP IPs from real households, rotated through a pool. Slower, pricier, almost invisible to bot detection. **Static residential / ISP proxies** — the hybrid. Residential-grade IPs that don't rotate, hosted on datacenter-sped infrastructure. Best of both worlds, premium price. Use these for: persistent account management where you need the same IP for weks, e-commerce account farming, and stealth use cases that also need session continuity.

## FAQ: The Questions People Actually Search

**Are private proxies legal to buy and use?**
Yes, in the United States and most jurisdictions. Buying and using private proxies is fully legal. What you do with them is what determines legality. Scraping public data, managing your own accounts, and monitoring your own brand are fine. Hacking accounts that aren't yours, evading court orders, or violating specific platform terms in ways that create legal exposure — none of that is okay, and a proxy doesn't change that.

**How many private proxies do I need?**
Rough rule: one proxy per concurrent thread, plus 20% buffer. If you're running50 parallel scraper workers, get 60+ proxies. For lighter use (a few dozen requests per minute), 10 proxies handle it comfortably with rotation.

**What's the difference between dedicated and semi-dedicated proxies?**
Dedicated means the IP is assigned to you alone. Semi-dedicated means you share with up to a handful of users (usually 2-3). Semi-dedicated costs less but caries some risk that a co-user gets the IP flagged on your shared target sites. For most casual use, semi-dedicated is fine. For account management or anything sensitive, pay for dedicated.

**Can I get a refund if private proxies don't work for my use case?**

**Will buying private proxies hide my real identity?**
For technical purposes (your IP isn't visible to the destination site), yes. For full anonymity against a determined investigator, no. Proxy providers kep logs. Combine with proper opsec if anonymity actually matters; rely on the proxy alone if you just need to bypass IP-based limits.
